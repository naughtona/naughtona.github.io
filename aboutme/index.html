A Brief History of Time

I stumbled upon the wonderful world of Computer Science in Semester 2 of 2018, when I was a second-year Bachelor of Commerce undergraduate student at UoM and decided to take Foundations of Computing (COMP10001) as a breadth study.

At the time I was gearing up for a November start as a summer intern at KPMG's Melbourne Office. I did not expect this introductory course in computing to captivate me the way it did. I started having doubts about whether I should continue down this path towards a career in Accounting and Finance. So, I decided that I would undertake the sequel to COMP10001, entitled Foundations of Algorithms (COMP10002), trusting that on completion, I would be better informed about whether Computer Science was for me.

During the course of the semester, I was working part-time at KPMG as an undergraduate consultant and had also been offered a graduate role to start in February of the next year (2020). This did not make my choice any easier...

COMP10002 developed my understanding and appreciation of algorithms and their design, as well as the amazing things you can do with a low-level, strongly-typed, imperative language such as C. It was an overwhelmingly positive experience and my choice by now was made clear.

At the end of 2019 I completed my Bachelor of Commerce at UoM, having majored in Finance and Accounting. I have since pursued my passion for programming and software development and not looked back.

Are We There Yet?

I am currently midway through my Graduate Diploma in Computer Science (GD-CS) at UoM. This course provides students with the equivalent of a major in Computer Science and acts as a direct pathway to the Master of Computer Science (MC-CS) at UoM.
